Laguna Niguel, California

Laguna Niguel, located in Orange County, California, is a picturesque city nestled in the San Joaquin Hills near the Pacific Ocean. Known for its master-planned community origins, the city was established in 1959 and incorporated in 1989. It boasts a mild coastal climate, a low crime rate, and abundant open spaces, including parks and trails that make it a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. With a population of over 64,000, Laguna Niguel serves as a residential hub for professionals commuting to job centers in northern and central Orange County. The city's name derives from "Laguna" (Spanish for lagoon) and "Niguili," a Native American village once located near Aliso Creek.

History

Laguna Niguel is a city in Orange County, California, that has a rich and diverse history. The name of the city comes from the Spanish words for "lagoon" and "Niguili", which was the name of a Native American village that once existed near Aliso Creek. The area was originally part of the Rancho Niguel land grant, which was given to Juan Avila by the Mexican government in 1842. Avila was a rancher who raised sheep and cattle on his land until 1865, when he sold it to Lewis Moulton, an American businessman who expanded the ranch to over 19,000 acres.

In 1959, the Laguna Niguel Corporation was founded by Cabot, Cabot and Forbes, a real estate company that aimed to develop one of the first master-planned communities in California. They hired Victor Gruen and Associates, a renowned urban planning firm, to create a detailed community plan for the 7,100-acre site. The plan envisioned a balanced and sustainable community that would preserve the natural beauty and open space of the area, while providing residential, commercial, recreational, and educational facilities for the residents. The plan also included a network of parks and trails, a civic center, a regional shopping mall and a lake.

The first land sales in Laguna Niguel began in 1961, with the Monarch Bay and Laguna Terrace subdivisions. The development of the community continued throughout the 1960s and 1970s, attracting more homebuyers and businesses to the area. In 1971, Avco Community Developer acquired the Laguna Niguel Plan from the Laguna Niguel Corporation and continued to implement it according to the original vision. By 1980, Laguna Niguel had a population of about 12,000 people.

In the 1980s, Laguna Niguel residents began to express their desire for local governance and control over their community affairs. They formed a Community Services District in 1986, which provided some municipal services such as street lighting, landscaping, and animal control. In 1989, after a successful campaign by the Laguna Niguel Cityhood Committee, Laguna Niguel residents voted overwhelmingly to incorporate it as a city. On December 1, 1989, Laguna Niguel became the 29th city in Orange County.

Today, Laguna Niguel is a thriving and prosperous city with a population of over 64,000 people. It is known for its mild coastal climate, low crime rate and high quality of life. It has over one-third of its land designated as open space, which includes two county regional parks, several neighborhood parks, a dog park, and a skate park. It also has a variety of cultural and recreational amenities, such as the Sea Country Senior and Community Center, the Crown Valley Community Park and Pool, the Niguel Botanical Preserve, and the Laguna Niguel Regional Lake. Laguna Niguel is proud of its history and heritage and continues to strive for excellence as a model master-planned community.

Things To Do

Nestled in the stunning landscape of Orange County, Laguna Niguel offers a blend of tranquility, adventure, and vibrant community life. Whether you're a nature enthusiast, a foodie, or just someone looking to soak up the Southern California vibe, this master-planned community has something for everyone.

Outdoor Adventures

Laguna Niguel is a paradise for outdoor lovers. Start your day at the Laguna Niguel Regional Park, where you can enjoy scenic trails, a peaceful lake, and perfect picnic spots. For a more rugged adventure, head to the Aliso & Wood Canyons Wilderness Park, offering miles of trails surrounded by lush greenery and opportunities to spot local wildlife. Don't forget your camera—views here are breathtaking!

Coastal Excursions

Just a short drive from Laguna Niguel, you'll find spectacular beaches like Doheny State Beach and Crystal Cove State Park, perfect for swimming, surfing, or simply lounging under the sun. These coastal gems are great for families, solo travelers, or groups of friends looking for some ocean therapy.

Community Experiences

Immerse yourself in the local culture at the Laguna Niguel Farmer’s Market, held weekly. Stroll through vibrant stalls offering fresh produce, handcrafted goods, and delectable treats. It's a fantastic place to meet locals and soak in the city's friendly atmosphere.

Arts and Cultrue

While Laguna Niguel itself is peaceful, nearby Laguna Beach is a haven for art enthusiasts. Visit charming galleries or the Murphy-Smith Historical Bungalow, a slice of history amidst the area's modern allure. Whether you're seeking inspiration or simply admiring talent, this adds an artistic touch to your exploration.

Hidden Gems

If you're looking for something truly unique, explore Laguna Niguel's lesser-known treasures like community-organized events and neighborhood parks. These spots capture the essence of why so many fall in love with the city—its vibrant yet calming vibe and strong sense of community.

Schools

Laguna Niguel, a serene and family-friendly city in Orange County, California, is home to a variety of educational institutions that cater to students of all ages. From elementary schools to private academies, the city offers a robust selection of options for families seeking quality education.

Elementary Schools

Moulton Elementary School

Moulton Elementary School is a National Blue Ribbon and California Distinguished School. Known for its strong academic programs and hands-on science labs, Moulton Elementary provides a nurturing environment for young learners.

Hidden Hills Elementary School

Hidden Hills Elementary School emphasizes a well-rounded curriculum and community involvement.

Middle Schools

Niguel Hills Middle School

Niguel Hills Middle School offers a comprehensive academic program alongside extracurricular activities such as music, sports, and clubs, fostering both intellectual and personal growth.

High Schools

While Laguna Niguel itself does not have a public high school within its city limits, students often attend nearby schools in the Capistrano Unified School District, such as Dana Hills High School.

Private Schools

St. Anne School

St. Anne School, serving students from kindergarten through eighth grade, is renowned for its rigorous academics and emphasis on character development.

Laguna Niguel Junior Academy

Niguel Junior Academy offers a Christian-based curriculum for kindergarten through eighth grade.

Colleges and Higher Education

While Laguna Niguel does not have colleges or universities within its boundaries, its proximity to institutions like Saddleback College in Mission Viejo provides residents with access to higher education opportunities. Saddleback College offers a wide range of programs, from associate degrees to vocational training.
