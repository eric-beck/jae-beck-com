San Clemente, California

In 1925, a former mayor of Seattle named Ole Hanson bought a portion of the ranch and envisioned creating a planned community that would preserve the natural beauty of the area. He named his project San Clemente after San Clemente Island, which is visible from the shore on a cloudless day. He envisioned San Clemente as a "Spanish Village by the Sea." He imagined a community that would reflect the Mediterranean-style architecture and ambiance of coastal Spain, a vision that has been preserved and can still be seen in the city's design and layout today. Hanson hired architects to design homes and buildings in a uniform Spanish colonial revival style, with red tile roofs, white stucco walls, and arched windows and doors. He also built a public beach club, a golf course, a pier, and a train station to attract visitors and residents.

History

San Clemente is a coastal city in Orange County, California, known for its Spanish colonial-style architecture and scenic beaches. The land that is now San Clemente was originally part of the Rancho San Onofre y Santa Margarita, a large cattle ranch granted to Pio Pico, the last Mexican governor of Alta California, in 1841. After the Mexican American War, Pico sold his land to John Forster, an English immigrant who married Pico's sister-in-law. Forster continued to raise cattle and horses on the ranch until his death in 1882.

San Clemente soon became a popular destination for celebrities, politicians, and tourists who enjoyed its mild climate and picturesque views. Some of the famous people who stayed or lived in San Clemente include Hollywood stars like Charlie Chaplin, Betty Grable, and Mickey Rooney; President Franklin D. Roosevelt, who visited Hanson's home in 1938; and President Richard Nixon, who bought a seaside estate in 1969 and dubbed it "La Casa Pacifica" or "The Western White House". Nixon hosted several foreign dignitaries at his San Clemente retreat, such as Soviet leader Leonid Brezhnev and Chinese leader Mao Zedong.

San Clemente has also faced some challenges and changes over the years. In 1939, a major flood damaged many homes and businesses in the downtown area. In 1971, an earthquake caused some structural damage to buildings and cracked the pier. In 1983, a landslide destroyed several homes along the coast. In 1993, the nearby San Onofre Nuclear Generating Station was shut down after a leak was detected in one of its reactors. In 2005, the city adopted a historic preservation ordinance to protect its architectural heritage from demolition or alteration.

San Clemente's history is not just about its architecture; it's also about a community that values outdoor living and recreation. With its ideal climate boasting approximately 300 days of sunshine per year and an average annual temperature of 73 degrees, residents and visitors alike enjoy a variety of outdoor activities. The city offers 25 parks, 6.8 miles of ridgeline trails, 2.3 miles of coastal trails, several championship golf courses, and over 20 acres of beautiful beaches. Surfing, in particular, is a core aspect of San Clemente's culture, reflecting the city's embrace of its stunning coastal environment.

Today, San Clemente is home to about 65,000 people who enjoy its laid-back lifestyle and cultural diversity. The city hosts several annual events, such as the Ocean Festival, the Fiesta Street Fair, and the Surfing Heritage and Culture Center's Surfboard Auction. The city also boasts several attractions, such as Casa Romantica Cultural Center and Gardens, San Clemente State Beach, and Trestles Beach. San Clemente is a city that honors its past while embracing its present and future.

Things To Do

City offers a variety of activities that cater to different interests. Whether it's exploring the charming downtown area, indulging in water sports, or simply relaxing by the sea, San Clemente provides a memorable experience for visitors and locals alike.

Casa Romantica Cultural Center and Gardens

Nestled atop a bluff overlooking the Pacific Ocean, this historic landmark, once the home of San Clemente's founder Ole Hanson, has been transformed into a vibrant cultural hub that offers a plethora of activities and experiences for visitors of all ages.

The center boasts a diverse array of programs. The beautifully preserved Spanish architecture of the estate provides a stunning backdrop for the center's events, while the 2.5 acres of lush, water-wise gardens offer a serene retreat. Casa Romantica Cultural Center and Gardens is a jewel in Southern California's cultural crown.

San Clemente Coastal Trail

This 2.3-mile trail, stretching along the picturesque coastline of Orange County, is a haven for those seeking a serene escape into nature. The trail is well-maintained and accessible for walkers, joggers, and cyclists, making it a versatile path for various activities. The trail is open from 4 AM to midnight, with the southern segment from the south T-Street restrooms to Calafia Beach operating from 6 AM to 10 PM. Dogs are welcome on the trail, provided they are on a leash no longer than 6 feet, ensuring a safe environment for all trail users. A highlight of the trail is the "Pier Bowl".

San Clemente Pier

Originally built in 1928, the pier has a rich history, including a period during Prohibition when it was used by bootleggers to smuggle alcohol. Despite being damaged by storms over the years, the pier has been rebuilt and stands today as a 1,296-foot-long wooden structure that welcomes tourists and locals alike.

Visitors can enjoy fishing, dining at oceanfront restaurants, or simply taking a leisurely stroll to soak in the stunning coastal views. The pier is also a popular spot for watching surfers navigate the waves to the north, while swimmers and bodysurfers often prefer the south side. With its ideal weather year-round, the pier is a perfect destination for those looking to experience the charm of the "Spanish Village by the Sea."

San Clemente Ocean Festival

A Celebration of Sea, Sand, and Surf also known as "The Greatest Show on Surf," is annual event that brings together the community, athletes, and families for a weekend of ocean-themed activities and competitions. It dates back to 1977. Next celebration will be July 20th and 21st, marking its 46th anniversary.

It began as a local lifeguard competition and had since evolved into ocean safety, environmental awareness, and community spirit. Participants and spectators can look forward to the world-famous Dory boat races, a 5K beach run, one-mile ocean swim, and the 'Groms Rule' Surf Contest. For the younger crowd, there's the Dolphin Dash kid's beach run and a variety of youth and family events, including a fishing derby and sand sculpting contest. The festival also features a free beach concert, Woodie Car Exhibits, and an Ocean Art Show, ensuring that there's something for everyone

San Clemente Art Festival

This festival is a haven for art enthusiasts, collectors, and families, offering a chance to enjoy a wide array of handcrafted art and unique creations.

At the heart of the festival is the Village Art Faire, a tradition that transforms the local community center and library lawns into an outdoor gallery where Southern California's most talented artisans showcase their work. From stunning photography and fine art to intricate jewelry, woodwork, and ceramics, the festival is a showcase of innovation and craftsmanship.

San Clemente Downtown

A stroll down Avenida Del Mar offers an array of experiences, from boutique shopping to fine dining. Visitors can indulge in the flavors of local cuisine at Nick's San Clemente or enjoy the fresh Mexican fare at South of Nick's Mexican Kitchen + Bar. For those with a sweet tooth, delightful desserts await at the many cafes lining the streets.

The district boasts a 4.5-star rating from visitors, reflecting its appeal as a destination that caters to both locals and tourists alike.
