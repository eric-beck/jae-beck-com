Mission Viejo, California

Mission Viejo is a city in Orange County, California, which is known for being one of the largest master-planned communities in the United States.

History

The history of Mission Viejo begins with Rancho Mission Viejo, a vast Spanish land grant that covered about 200,000 acres of what is now southern Orange County. The land was originally part of the Mission San Juan Capistrano, which was founded in 1776 by Spanish friars. However, after Mexico gained independence from Spain in 1821, the missions were secularized, and their lands were divided among various owners. One of these owners was Don Juan Forster, an English-born Mexican ranchero who purchased Rancho Mission Viejo in 1845 for $14,000. Forster used the land for cattle and sheep grazing and provided horses to the United States military during the Mexican American War. Forster's descendants continued to own and operate the ranch until 1963, when they sold it to a group of developers led by Donald Bren. The developers who bought Rancho Mission Viejo had a grand vision for transforming the hilly and rugged terrain into a modern and attractive community. They formed the Mission Viejo Company, which was headed by Richard O'Neill Jr., Jerome Moiso, and Anthony Moiso. They hired urban planner Donald Bren to design a master plan for the new city, which would be named after the original ranch. Bren's plan was inspired by the concept of "New Towns", which were planned communities that aimed to provide a balanced and integrated environment for living, working, and recreation. Bren envisioned Mission Viejo as a city that would preserve the natural beauty and character of the land, while offering a variety of housing options, amenities, and services to its residents. He also incorporated elements of Spanish architecture and landscaping to reflect the city's heritage. The Mission Viejo Company began construction of the city in 1965, starting with the development of Lake Mission Viejo, a man-made reservoir that would serve as a focal point and recreational center for the community. The company also built roads, schools, parks, shopping centers, golf courses, and other facilities to support the growing population. The first homes were sold in 1966, and by 1972, more than 10,000 people lived in Mission Viejo. As Mission Viejo continued to expand and prosper, its residents began to express their desire for more local control and autonomy over their community. In 1987, they voted to incorporate it as a city, becoming the 27th city in Orange County. The city adopted a council-manager form of government, with five elected council members and a hired city manager. Since its incorporation, Mission Viejo has maintained its reputation as one of the most desirable places to live in Orange County and in California. The city has been recognized for its high quality of life, low crime rate, excellent schools, abundant parks and trails, diverse cultural and recreational opportunities, and strong community spirit. The city has also hosted several national and international sporting events, such as the U.S. Olympic Festival in 1989 and the U.S. Senior Open Golf Championship in 1997. Today, Mission Viejo has a population of about 94,000 people and covers an area of eighteen square miles. It is mostly residential in nature and culture, but also has some offices and businesses. The city is still noted for its tree-lined neighborhoods and its adherence to its original master plan. The city's motto is "Make Living Your Mission", which reflects its commitment to providing a fulfilling and enriching experience for its residents.

Things To Do

Mission Viejo is a beautiful city in Orange County, California, which offers a variety of attractions and activities for visitors and residents alike. Whether you are looking for outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, shopping opportunities, or family fun, you will find something to enjoy in Mission Viejo. Here are some of the things you can do in this charming city: Visit the Mission Viejo Lake: This man-made lake is the centerpiece of the city and a popular destination for boating, fishing, swimming, and picnicking.
